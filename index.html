Discography

The discography of Nick Drake, an English folk musician and singer-songwriter, consists of three studio albums. Drake released his debut studio album, Five Leaves Left, in July 1969 and received poor marketing from Island and mixed reviews from critics. In March 1971, Drake released his second studio album, Bryter Layter, which featured a more up-tempo and jazz influenced sound. In October 1971, Drake began recording sessions with a new producer, John Wood. Recorded at two midnight sessions at Sound Techniques in London, the sessions resulted in Drake's third and final studio album, Pink Moon. It was released in February 1972 and despite poor sales, Pink Moon received positive critical acclaim.
